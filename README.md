📊 IBM HR Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

🔍 Overview
This project showcases an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard built using Power BI, based on the widely used IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset.
The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of employee demographics, job roles, compensation, and organizational structure, helping derive actionable HR insights.

🎯 Objectives
Analyze workforce distribution across multiple dimensions
Understand employee demographics and diversity
Explore salary and job role patterns
Identify trends in education, department, and travel behavior
Enable data-driven HR decision-making

📁 Dataset
Source: IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset
Contains employee-level data including:
Age, Gender
Hourly Rate
Job Role & Department
Education Field
Marital Status
Business Travel

📊 Dashboard Features
👥 Employees by Age
Histogram showing age distribution
Majority of employees fall in the 30–40 age group
🚻 Employees by Gender
Donut chart representation
Gender split: 60% Male | 40% Female
💰 Employees by Hourly Rate
Displays variation in employee compensation
Helps identify pay distribution patterns
💼 Employees by Job Role
Sales Executive, Research Scientist, and Laboratory Technician dominate
Useful for workforce role analysis
🎓 Employees by Education Field
Majority from Life Sciences and Medical backgrounds
Highlights hiring trends
🏢 Employees by Department
Research & Development has the largest workforce
Followed by Sales and Human Resources
💍 Employees by Marital Status
Distribution across Married, Single, and Divorced
Useful for demographic segmentation
✈️ Employees by Business Travel
Most employees fall under Travel Rarely
Indicates limited travel requirements for majority roles

📈 Key Insights
Workforce is concentrated in mid-age professionals (30–40)
Male employees form the majority (~60%)
R&D dominates organizational structure
Employees largely come from Life Sciences backgrounds
Business travel is not frequent for most employees
